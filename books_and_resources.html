@ I have set up the strips with some colleagues in Chile but I'm not sure if they have dug them up or measured the results yet. The American biofertiliser guys like Jairo Restrepo and Eugenio Gras have a tool for capturing wild soil and forest microbes and then bringing them into your site to replenish you soil microbiology. It is basically an agar plate made from potato starch which is buried in an intact area and then colonized. After some time you dig it up and use it to colonize sawdust or another substrate which you can incorporate into your site. Wild stuff!
