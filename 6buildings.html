I really like metal insulated panels for steel buildings - essentially these are a sheet of foam insulation sandwiched between two sheets of metal with snap to fit connections. These can be used for both roofs and walls. Installation is fast and easy. R values can be as high as R42 (6" thick). This is what I'm using on the metal framed greenhouses and support buildings for the non-glazed surfaces.

2017-07-26 13:51

Finishing usually will take more time but that varies greatly based on what you are doing with the building.

@ The fence will be no problem for keeping the chickens in. But what predators do you have? I'd say without a night coop or other covered/sealed-in enclosure, brace yourself for heavy losses from night predators. That may change if you have a couple of good guardian dogs (raised with the chickens) - indeed @ and others very likely know more about this. Shade will definitely be an issue in the higher range of those temps. We were raising cornish x meat birds on pasture and in the low to mid-30s they started to keel over (late May to mid September in Okanagan, BC). Free access to water *that they don't have to walk far to get to* and far = less than about 5 m. We had a setup of mobile nipples and automatic waterers (small refilling bowls) hooked to a hose, which kept the water from getting too hot or mucky in hot weather, and flowing frequently enough to keep it from heating up in the hose.
