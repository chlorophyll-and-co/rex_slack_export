regrarians
2017-07-28 23:57
Great question ? the difference is time and surface condition @kimo. The HSG values are considering that the soil has reached field capacity and that say 48% would mean that is what you would get as an upper level of runoff on that soil type on that land use during a peak rainfall event. This is so that you can use the HSG value to determine peak flow volumes off of an area in a given time for that rainfall event. From that figure, usually in cubic metres per second (cumecs) or cubic feet per second (cfs) you can then determine the sizing of drains and pipes or any other water control feature within that catchment. The values in Nelson's chart is all about calculating the volume of runoff from a catchment over a decade. This then helps you to determine how much water is available as runoff for planning dams and other storages.
