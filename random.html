2017-07-07 18:39
I use my bones to make bone broths (I add a touch of vinegar to pull minerals out of the bones and let it go low and slow - 180 degrees F for 8 hrs), and then after making broth twice (the 1st batch is for soups, the second lighter batch is for making beans or rice, in place of using water why not use stock?), I give them to dogs, or bury them in the soil. Crushing them to make bone meal also seams like a good option. I have friends who use them to make biochar to increase fertility in the soil. this is a process of burning the bones till they are all black like charcoal for grilling. Look up Biochar, and I'm sure we will be discussing it in the #soils channel. Other people who cook them into a bone sauce to ward of deer and other wildlife, Sepp Holzer's Bone Sauce being the most famous, and I even know ranchers who ask their Meat CSA customers for the bones back, in order to return them to the land. other times, I decorate my cactus garden, or my farm to table dinners with them. Femurs can be cut into candle holders or shot glasses.
