2017-08-13 20:21
Hello everyone - we are in the planning stages for building a anaerobic digester (300 m3), and will use Opuntia cactus (prickly pear, nopales) as the feedstock. We plan to start with 6 hectares of the nopales, planted at ~ 20,000 per hectare. The areas we'll plan to use to plant the nopales have relatively poor soil and are relatively steep, but we hope will still be workable if we plant in terraces. We are working with Elqui Global Energy for the planning and installation. The projections are to be producing 800 tons of nopal per hectare per year (by year 3), which will produce about 350,000 m3 of biogas (about 60% methane), and with a 100 kW generator, will generate about 950,000 kWh per year. Some of the gas and electricity will be used for the residences on the property and irrigation pumps, though most of the electricity will be sold to a neighboring business. If anyone has an experience with building or operating an anaerobic digesters, and growing your own feedstock for it, I would be really interested to hear the details.

2017-08-13 20:53
I am developing a biogas plant in Cobleskill NY that will mostly be using manure from dairy and feedlots. Are you working with a reputable biogas vendor? I strongly doubt that 800 tons of feed stock will fuel a 100KW generator - Bioferm has a containerized 100KW system http://www.biofermenergy.com/anaerobic-digestion-technology/eucolino_small-scale-digester/ that needs 6000 tons of feed stock per year. Also, your KWH numbers do not add up -- 100KW x 24 hours x 365 days = 876,000 KWH. However, this is too high as you need to account for down time for maintenance.

2017-08-13 20:55
Also, a 100KW digester system will likely cost between $500,000 and $800,000. The lowest figures would be $3500 per KW but that is only achievable on larger systems (500 KW to MW scale).

2017-08-13 21:00
Finally, I would do more research about the use of cactus as feed stock - this is one I have never seen used as a feed stock. I would be concerned that the energy content is low. I would also be concerned about the need for pre-processing and the total labor costs. Biogas digester economics work best when the feedstock is free and is an existing waste stream.

2017-08-13 22:06
