Live2 Q&A <#C625UCG4E|week6> "So, in terms of drought proofing for water for stock how many years' reserve do you recommend? 2 years which covers off a dry year scenario?" @ Answered at 1:43:30. (Yes). Best way to ensure a 2-3 year water storage capacity is building in depth (with small surface area), and having multiple storages to rely on. Reduce numbers early to avoid feeding/watering stock into drought; keep stock watered off the storage (e.g. in troughs) to keep water storage clean and supply to stock fresh. During a drought, as water levels go down, turbidity increases and quality declines; can add to stress, illness in stock already stressed by declining forage quality. Keeping water clean is one of the easier ways to keep animals (and people) healthy.
