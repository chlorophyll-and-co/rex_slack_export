Being relatively extensive @ I think that a shift to holistic planned grazing would be the best way to go. Native grass seed is very expensive ($4-500+/kg) and establishing them before your site and management is ready has the potential to be very costly indeed. Its likely that there will be remnants here and there, particularly on the edges and roadsides/public land and so even though the previous management of constant cultivation and pre-emergent/selective herbicides etc. has gone on you would over time get some recruitment. The other thing to consider is undertaking a pasture/cover cropping program. This would accelerate the development of soil carbon, something that the better quality perennial native grasses require to establish.
