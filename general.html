Essentially, rasters are good for photographs, paintings, things that you are not likely to edit separate shapes in. Which brings me to vectors - they are basically shapes. You can click on each line individually and move it around, alter its properties, etc., so it makes it easier if you are doing things like keyline for example. Also vectors are mathematical calculations rather than just solid squares, so they can be enlarged and scaled down endlessly. Hence, vectors are used for building plans, logos (if you need to print them on a building), t-shirt prints, etc. Hope that helps.
